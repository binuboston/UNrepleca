  1. Who is eligible to participate in this conference?

    The conference is open to students between the 8th and 12th grades.

  2. Will i get a certificate?

  4. What is the UN Replica Assembly for students?

    The UN Replica Assembly is a simulated United Nations General Assembly experience designed for students. Participants assume the roles of delegates representing different countries and engage in diplomatic debates to address global issues.

  5. Who can participate in the UN Replica Assembly?

    The Assembly is open to students from various educational institutions, including high schools, colleges, and universities. It welcomes students with an interest in international relations, diplomacy, and global affairs.

  6. How do register for the UN Replica Assembly?

    To register, visit our official Application and follow the registration instructions provided. Make sure to complete all required fields and submit the registration form before the deadline.

  7. Is there a registration fee for the Assembly?

    The registration fee, if applicable, will be merntioned on the Assembly website. Some Assemblies may offer early-bird discounts or scholarships for eligible participants.

  8. Can choose the country represent during the Assembly?

    Yes, during the registration process, you may have the opportunity to express your preference for the country you wish to represent. However, country assignments will be subject to availability and Assembly organizers' discretion.

  9. Will l receive a certificate for participating in the UN Replica Assembly?

    Yes, all registered participants will receive a certificate of participation, recognizing their engagerment and contribution to the Assembly.

  10. What topics will be discussed during the Assembly?

    The Assembly will focus on a range of global issues, similar to those addressed by the United Nations. Topics will cover areas such as peace and security, human rights, climate change, and sustainable development.
